Tips for active listening

Active listening is a communication technique that involves giving your full attention to the speaker, understanding their message, and responding thoughtfully. Some techniques to improve your listening are:

Paraphrasing-. Expressing the meaning of the idea in your own words to get clarification. Generally, done by asking questions.

Key Information-. To identify the main ideas and concepts of the conversation or context.

Be fully present-. Avoid unnecessary distraction to the conversation while the other person is talking. You can try to turn off your cellphone or close your laptop.

Common listening mistakes

Listening to reply: Many people formulate their answers while the other person is still talking. Avoid doing this, as it can cause many problems because you may miss essential information.

Stage hogging: Introducing your own ideas or opinions unnecessarily instead of focusing on the other person's problem. This shifts the attention away from them and makes them feel unheard.

Multitasking: Doing other activities while the other person is speaking. This can distract you, causing you to miss important information and making the other person feel that you are not paying enough attention.
