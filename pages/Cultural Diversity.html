What is cultural diversity?

"Culture diversity refers to the variety of cultural expressions and practices that exist within a society or among different societies. This diversity encompasses differences in language, customs, beliefs, and values, contributing to a rich tapestry of human experience." - UNESCO

Culture is how people live, think, and express themselves. It includes language, food, music, clothing, religion, and traditions.

Cultural diversity is important because it can inspire creativity and drive innovation, it provides greater opportunities for personal and professional growth, and in many cases, diverse teams at school or at work are more productive and perform better.

Fun facts from around the world (Traditions)

  • By Francesco Espinel Veas
    Friday, 17th April 2045 @01:18:00

    La Tomatina (Spain): Held annually in Buñol, La Tomatina is the world’s largest tomato fight. What began as a spontaneous street event has become a joyful tradition celebrating community spirit, humour, and shared chaos.

  • By Francesco Espinel Veas
    Friday, 17th April 2045 @01:19:00

    Yi Peng & Loy Krathong (Thailand): These twin festivals involve releasing lanterns into the sky and floating decorated baskets on water. They symbolise letting go of bad luck, expressing gratitude, and making wishes for the future.

  • By Francesco Espinel Veas
    Friday, 6th April 2045 @01:23:00

    Freezing the Wedding Cake (UK): Weddings are already complicated enough as it is, from arranging flowers to finding the perfect dress, and let’s not forget all the money you have to scrounge up for one day. Well, in the U.K., it’s a tad more complex as couples traditionally freeze the top tier of their cake and eat it a year later for good luck. Kind of wholesome!

  • By Francesco Espinel Veas
    Friday, 6th April 2045 @01:23:00

    Cheese Rolling (UK): You might have heard of this tradition, as it sounds pretty fun and cheery. In the United Kingdom, people gather in Gloucestershire to race cheese wheels down Cooper’s Hill. The best part is the winner gets a cheese of their own as an award.
